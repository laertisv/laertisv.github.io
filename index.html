About Me

From October 2021 to September 2024, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Department of Mathematical Sciences of the Norwegian University of Science and Technology, under the mentorship of Steffen Oppermann. Previously, I held a postdoctoral position at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn, mentored by Jan Schröer. I completed my PhD at the Department of Mathematics of Uppsala University, supervised by Martin Herschend. My research focuses on the representation theory of finite-dimensional algebras, particularly higher-dimensional Auslander–Reiten theory and the global dimension of algebras.

Research Interests

A crucial notion in higher-dimensional Auslander–Reiten theory is that of n-cluster tilting modules and subcategories. Due to the homological nature of this theory, the global dimension of the underlying algebra plays a significant role. My research aims to construct and classify n-cluster tilting modules and subcategories with interesting properties. I am particularly interested in the global dimension of algebras that admit such modules. To this end, I focus on special classes of algebras like Nakayama algebras, representation-directed algebras, and radical square zero algebras, employing tools such as algebra epimorphisms and categorical limits to construct algebras where the existence of an n-cluster tilting module can be confirmed, and the global dimension can be controlled.
